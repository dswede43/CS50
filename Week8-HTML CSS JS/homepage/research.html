Research

All research projects were conducted at the University of Ottawa (Ottawa, ON, Canada).

Tissue attachments

Undergraduate projects using histology to characterize bone-tissue attachments in rat and fish model organisms. Specifically, knee capsule attachments to the tibia and femur in Sprague-Dawley rats and the tendon attachment interface to vertebrae in Rainbow Trout fish.

The rat knee capsule project was possible from receiving an Undergraduate Research Opportunity Program (UROP) scholarship from uOttawa.

My honours thesis on fish tendon attachment received an award for best graduate student poster at the Ottawa-Carleton Institute of Biology (OCIB) research symposium.

Microgravity

My current research is studying the effect of microgravity on human transcriptomes by utilizing the R coding environment to apply generalized linear models on high-throughput RNA-sequencing expression datasets. This is to analyze the gene expression patterns in leukocytes isolated from participants subject to 60-days of bed rest (an Earth based microgravity analog) and astronauts aboard the International Space Station (ISS).

The analysis of participants at baseline prior to 60 days of bed rest was presented at the Toronto RNA Enthusiasts' Day (TREnD) symposium.
